Linear Regression model for Years and Total Arrested People



What is the relationship between the year and the number of total arrested people each year?

According to the total number of arrested people from 2008 to 2018, we are able to build a linear regression model. The slope of this model is -213862.32, which means there were about 213,862 cases decrease for each year on average. The model performance is limited by the size of the data, as we only have data for 11 years for this project in total. However, we believe this model could show an overall decreasing trend of the number of arrested people from 2008 to 2018. For the year of 2018, there were 6,576,654 people arrested, but there was a 25.8363% decrease compared with 8,867,758 people arrested in 2008.

Comparison between Male and Female Arrests (2008-2018)



How was the gender distribution of people arrested in previous years?

According to the stacked bar plot, we can see there were more male arrested than female counterparts. With the overall people arrested decreasing, the ratio of female to male arrested was increasing. For example, compared to the ratio of 0.329 in 2008, the year of 2018 had a higher ratio of 0.374. In the line plot beside, we can see a clear pattern that the ratio of female over male was increasing gradually over years.









Male Juvenile Arrests VS Female Juvenile Arrests



As we continue to explore the data we have, what would the distribution of juvenile and adult arrested be in different gender?

Number of Poeple Arrested and Ethnicity/Race



The goal is to assess the arrested trend over the years among different ethnic and racial groups. Which ethnicity has the highest change in percentage of arrested people, and which ethnic group has the lowest change in percentage of arrested people?

From the stacked-bar plot it is visible that for Whites the percentage of arrested people was 69.2% in 2008 while in 2018 it was 69%. For Blacks/African-Americans, there was a greater percentage change as it dropped from 28.3% in 2008 to 27.4% in 2018. However for American Indians/Alaska Natives this percentage grew from 1.3% in 2008 to 2.1% in 2018. There is a minute increase in the percentage of people arrested being Asians from 1.1% (2008) to 1.2% (2018). The percentage has vastly decreased among Native Hawaiians or Pacific Islanders from 1.1% in 2008 to 0.2% in 2018.

Therefore, The highest positive change in percentage of (0.9%) arrested people is shown by Native Hawaiians or Pacific Islanders and African Americans while there is an increase in percentage for Asians.

Conclusion

In conclusion, based on arrested data from 2008 to 2018 gathered and published by the Federal Bureau of Investigation, we are able to find patterns for different offences and for different social groups (age, race or gender).

We notice the top offences leading people to be arrested were always for larceny-theft, violence crime, property crime,drug abuse violations, driving under the influence. Among all those offences, people arrested for drug abuse violations were increasing over years. This might indicate that there might be more people who had drug abuse in general, or it might be that the law enforcement paid more attention to check if people had drug abuse than before. We believe this would be an interesting pattern for scholars or people who are interested to research on. Moreover, the government or the law enforcement could also persuade people to avoid drug abuse in the media in response to the increasing percentage of drug abuse violation among all offences.

We are delighted to find that the number of people arrested are decreasing, which might be a good indicator of public security. In addition, there is a positive correlation between state population and the number of people arrested in the state. Over the last 10 years, California has constantly been identified as the state with the largest number of arrests in the United States. This raises questions on the authorities responsible for controlling crime in California. The aligns with the correlation we mentioned above. In addition it also raises concerns regarding the safety of the citizens.

As for subgroups in the society, we find that there were more males arrested than females, while the ratio of female to male is increasing. People under 18 are less likely to be arrested for driving under the influence, as they have limited access to automobiles.

We believe these findings would benefit sociologists and people who are interested in crime data or law enforcement.
