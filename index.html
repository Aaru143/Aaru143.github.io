Introduction

Hello, Welcome to our website! This project is focusing on U.S. Arrested Data from 2008-2018 that has been collected by FBI(Federal Bureau Of Investigation) as a part of their UCR(Uniform Crime Reporting) Program. The program serves as the national repository for crime data collected by law enforcement. To know more about the data please navigate to the Data Page on our website

Our Objective :

The primary goal of our project is to analyze the crime data collected by vizualizing it and study the underlying patterns and trends. We have tried visualizing the trend of number of people arrested each year from 2008 to 2018 in United States Of America and see how they relate to different factors like geographical region, gender, race/ethnicity and age.

Questions Answered :

Some general questions - answers to which can be found on Results page are - How has the crime rate changed in different states in United States of America from 2008 to 2018? How does age play a role crime rate? Are juvenilles more likely to commit crimes in United States? Is Homicide one of the top crimes in the country due to gun-control laws? Are men more likely to commit crimes? How the vast ethnic compositon of the population of United States affect the criminal activities? What are some general trends between various factors?

Stakeholders :

In addition to helping general American public, our analyzes is also useful for criminologists, sociologists, legislators, city planners, the media and students of criminal justics system. The results from this project can be used to study variety of trends and for research/planning purposes.
